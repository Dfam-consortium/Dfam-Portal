About Dfam

Dfam is an open database of repetitive DNA elements organized around multiple sequence alignments of TE families. The seed alignment provides a model-neutral representation of a sequence family from which both a traditional consensus sequence and a profile hidden Markov model (HMM) may be built. The early releases of Dfam used RepeatMasker and the TE database, Repbase to bootstrap the generation of these seed alignments. For newly sequenced genomes, seed alignments may be obtained directly from de-novo repeat finders such as RepeatModeler.

Dfam version 3.1 was produced at the Institute for Systems Biology and the University of Montana and contains full TE libraries for five model organisms: human [hg38], mouse [mm10], zebrafish [danRer10], worm [ce10], and fly [dm6] and partial libraries for seven additional species.

Dfam is growing. We are embarking on an effort to greatly expand the database using de-novo repeat identification pipelines, data sharing with other open-databases, and most importantly from direct community submissions. If you have existing TE libraries or plan to develop one for a newly sequenced organism, consider making it a part of the Dfam database. We can offer assistance with importing legacy datasets and are working on tools to facilitate community curation of the database. Please contact us at help@dfam.org.

Dfam is freely available under the Creative Commons Zero ("CC0") license. This includes both the data and the software running the site (https://github.com/Dfam-consortium). We welcome anyone wishing to contribute.

Dfam also makes use of various third-party libraries, which have their own license terms. The up-to-date list of these libraries is present in the Dfam-Portal project on GitHub in the package.json file.

Dfam is powered by nhmmer, which is a part of the HMMER3 package and was written by Travis Wheeler (University of Montana) and Sean Eddy (HHMI, Harvard University).
