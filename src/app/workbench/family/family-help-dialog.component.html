Help

Info

General information about the repat family.

Name
(optional) A short, name of the family.

Examples: MIR, AluJ, Charlie2b.

Title
A short title or description for the family. Shown on the Browse page as the family Description. Shown on the family page as a subtitle under the Name.

Example: "Non-autonomous Internal region of HERV52, LTR52-int subfamily."

Description
A longer, publicly visible description of the family. Appropriate for details such as similarities to other families, explanations of function or structure, and other interesting facts.
Classification
The hierarchical classification of the family. Both generic ("LINE", "LTR", "SINE") and specific ("Zenon", "Helitron-1", "tRNA_Promoter;Deu-core;L2-end") classifications are available.
Taxa
Clade(s) the family belongs to. This is usually a single clade at which the repeat was introduced into the genome, except in the case of horizontal transfer between lineages. Dfam searches and visualizations assume the repeat family might be present in all descendants of the specified clade(s). Adding or removing clades from this list will lead to corresponding species being removed from graphs and statistics in the next full database release.

Search Options

Metadata that changes how the family is searched by Dfam and RepeatMasker.

Disabled
Disabled families are not visible on the website, not exported in data formats such as HMM or EMBL, nor are they counted in statistics or adjudication. However they are not deleted: they are still accessible via public database releases and can be un-disabled easily.
Search Stages
Used by RepeatMasker. This affects when the repeat family is cut or masked in relation to other repeat families.
Buffer Stages
Used by RepeatMasker. See the RepeatMasker paper for more details.

Curation Details

Dfam-specific details about the family's curation status and history.

Curation Status
The state of the family record in Dfam. Families usually move from Submitted to either Accepted or Rejected, and from Accepted to Released.
Length
The length in nucleotides of the family's seed alignment, consensus sequence, and HMM.
Target Site Duplication
The target site duplication for this family, if it is known. Expressed as nucleotide bases.

Examples: 'NNNNNNNN', 'TATGC'

Curation Notes
Additional context for the family. These notes are not displayed on the website on in exported data formats such as HMM or EMBL, but they are accessible via public database releases.

References

References to external sources with additional or alternative information about the same family.

Citations
References to articles where the family has been published, discussed, or described. Currently only accepts PubMed IDs.
Aliases
References to the same family in other databases.

Source

The origination and provenance of the family.

Submitter
The original uploader of the family to Dfam.
Author(s)
The authors of the family.
